<h2>

Instructions

</h2><p><strong>Preparation</strong>
</p><p>Stand or sit.
</p><p><strong>Execution</strong>
</p><p>Pull head back as far possible while looking slightly down and hold stretch.
</p>

<h2>Comments</h2><p>Neck retraction primarily consists of <a href="https://exrx.net/Articulations/Spine#Flexion">Cervical Flexion</a> (spine) accompanied by subtle <a href="https://exrx.net/Articulations/Spine#Flexion1">Thoracic Flexion</a> (spine) and <a href="https://exrx.net/Articulations/Neck#Flexion">Neck Flexion</a> (atlanto-occipital joint). Cervical spine has natural lordotic curvature (see <a href="https://exrx.net/Injury/LowBackTidbits">diagram of spine</a>). Much of the retraction motion is actually flattening or slight reversing the normal lordosis. The deep neck flexors perform majority of this movement. Slight neck Flexion at the atlanto-occipital joint also occurs to maintain forward head positioning. </p><p>Also see similar exercise <a href="https://exrx.net/WeightExercises/Splenius/LyingIsometricNeckRetr">Lying Neck Retraction Isometric</a>. </p><h2>Muscles</h2><p>Target
